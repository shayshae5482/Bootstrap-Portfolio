Insurance Trivia Game

Can we agree that insurance is a pretty boring topic? But it's the one asset that everyone needs. Whether you're buing a car, a home, computer equipment, or traveling to a different city, everyone wants to know if something goes wrong, they have something in place to protect them.

But when most consumers think of insurance, they think price first, coverage second. This is why you see so many "Save $500 when you switch to our insurance" ads. They work, if you're looking for price shoppers.

But studies show that an educated consumer are willing to purchase at a higher price and are more loyal to brands they trust.

So how do you get those types of customers? By creating an interactive experience that helps them learn while also being entertained.

This is why this app was created. It's a fun way to capture leads, build trust, and increase social media shares.

  • JQuery
  • Google Fonts
  • Bootstrap
  • HTML5
  • CSS

    The Ultimate Organizer

    In today's busy world, it's hard for most families to stay organized.

    That's why in this app, as a collaboration team, we decided to create a calendar app using React.Js.

    I was responsible for the front end design and overall color scheme. When I checked out competitor's landing pages, I saw a similar theme of selling features. They would say things like "accessible on all devices" and "perfect for families".

    But, there was something major missing: the end user's why.

    By doing a little customer research, I was able to extract what customer's were looking for in a calendar app. I mean, with so many free options available like Google Calendar, why wasn't that enough? What was missing? I took that information and created a design and landing page that spoke to those spoken (and unspoken) desires.

    Get GIPHY With It

    Let's have a little fun, shall we? In this app, I used the GIPHY API to create a fun experience for anyone who visits the page.

    By simply clicking on the buttons, a specific set of dancing images will appear in the browser. You can even add your very own buttons and they will magically (with the help of our good friend JQuery) appear on the webpage.

    Besides being fun, this is clever way to increase engagement on a webpage and reduce your site's bounce rate.

    I know they say "content is king" on the web. That is a true statement, but how that content is delivered should vary based on the target audience. In this case, this app is for a younger audience with a ton of energy.

    Crystal Collector Game

    Let's have some more fun.

    As a former teacher, I understand the importance of making learning fun. If you want to challenege someone's memory or knowledge, why not create a game to test them?

    According to UMASS-Amherst, Centre for Teaching and Faculty Development, games can be used to increase learning outcomes.

    In this particular app, your memory and math skills will be tested. What's even cooler is that this was built using what I call the Big 4: HTML, CSS, Javascript, and JQuery.
